US threatens sanctions against Chinese AI models over IP theft

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced that the U.S. might impose sanctions on Chinese open-source AI models, citing alleged intellectual property theft. This move expands the Trump administration's campaign to slow China's advancements in artificial intelligence.

Why this is happening
  • US aims to slow China's AI progress, viewing it as a strategic competitor.
  • Allegations of IP theft are a recurring theme in US-China tech disputes.
  • Sanctions would target specific open AI models, a new escalation tactic.
  • Prior administrations initiated efforts to restrict Chinese tech advances.
Possible knock-on effects
  • Likely to exacerbate US-China tech tensions and trade disputes.
  • Could force Chinese AI developers to rely more on domestic alternatives.
  • May pressure other nations to choose sides in the global AI competition.
  • Could impact international AI research collaboration and open-source sharing.

  • Likely to increase scrutiny on AI system capabilities and control.

Anthropic’s $1.5B copyright settlement approved; only 350 authors opted out

A federal judge approved Anthropic's $1.5 billion class action settlement with authors who accused the company of using copyrighted books for AI training. Around 350 authors chose not to participate.

Why this is happening
  • Anthropic was sued for allegedly training AI on copyrighted literary works.
  • The settlement aims to compensate authors for unauthorized use of material.
  • Only a small fraction of authors chose not to participate.
  • This marks a significant legal precedent in AI copyright cases.
Possible knock-on effects
  • Could establish a framework for future AI training data copyright claims.
  • May encourage other AI companies to proactively settle with content creators.
  • Likely to influence how AI models are trained and licensed moving forward.

OtherTechCrunch

Data centers expected to use 4x more electricity by 2035

Projections indicate that new data centers built through 2033 could consume as much electricity as India uses today, leading to a quadrupling of overall data center electricity usage by 2035.

Why this is happening
  • The rapid expansion of AI and cloud computing drives massive data center growth.
  • AI workloads are particularly energy-intensive, requiring significant power.
  • This trend raises concerns about global energy consumption and sustainability.
  • Existing infrastructure may struggle to meet future electricity demands.
Possible knock-on effects
  • Will likely spur innovation in energy-efficient hardware and cooling solutions.
  • Could lead to increased investment in renewable energy sources for data centers.
  • May pressure governments to develop new energy policies and infrastructure.
